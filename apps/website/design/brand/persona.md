# Target Personas

## Primary Persona A: The Hiring Manager

**Name:** Morgan (Engineering Manager/Technical Recruiter)

**Demographics:**
- Age range: 30-50
- Professional background: Engineering Manager, VP Engineering, Technical Recruiter, CTO
- Technical expertise level: Medium to High - can evaluate technical credentials and experience
- Industry: Tech companies, startups, established enterprises hiring for AI/ML roles

**Goals:**
- Vet candidates for senior AI/ML engineering or leadership positions
- Assess real-world experience beyond resume keywords
- Understand breadth and depth of technical expertise
- Evaluate cultural fit and communication skills through portfolio/writing
- Determine if candidate can bridge technical and business domains

**Pain Points:**
- Flooded with AI/ML resumes full of buzzwords but light on shipping experience
- Difficulty distinguishing genuine expertise from recent bootcamp grads
- Need to verify candidates can handle production systems, not just Kaggle competitions
- Looking for evidence of leadership, architecture, and product thinking
- Want to see how candidates communicate complex topics

**User Journey:**
1. **Discovery** - Finds site via LinkedIn, resume link, or candidate application
2. **Vetting** - Reviews project showcases to verify resume claims
3. **Depth Check** - Looks for 15+ years evidence, breadth across ML domains
4. **Communication Assessment** - Reads thought leadership to gauge communication skills
5. **Decision** - Reaches out for interview or moves to next stage

**What they need from the site:**
- Clear, verifiable evidence of shipping production ML systems
- Breadth of experience: generative AI, personalization, signal processing, architecture
- Demonstration of big-picture thinking and product sense
- Professional but authentic personality that suggests good cultural fit
- Easy contact information and links to LinkedIn/GitHub for verification

## Primary Persona B: The Technical Decision-Maker

**Name:** Alex (Technical Leader/Founder)

**Demographics:**
- Age range: 30-50
- Professional background: Tech leadership, startup founder, VP Engineering, CTO
- Technical expertise level: High - can evaluate technical depth and architectural decisions
- Industry: Tech startups, scale-ups, or established companies exploring AI/ML integration

**Goals:**
- Find experienced AI/ML engineers for technical advisory or leadership roles
- Understand what separates real ML experience from hype
- Evaluate thought leadership and technical depth before reaching out
- Discover new perspectives on AI/ML product development

**Pain Points:**
- Overwhelmed by AI/ML hype and surface-level expertise
- Difficulty finding engineers who can bridge research and production systems
- Need someone who understands both the technical depth AND business/product realities
- Skeptical of portfolios that don't show real-world shipping experience

**User Journey:**
1. **Discovery** - Finds site through GitHub, LinkedIn, conference talk, or search
2. **Evaluation** - Reads project showcases to assess technical depth and breadth
3. **Validation** - Checks 15+ years of experience, looks for concrete examples
4. **Connection** - Reaches out via contact info or social media links
5. **Return visits** - Follows for thought leadership content and new project updates

**What they need from the site:**
- Quick understanding of depth and breadth of experience
- Evidence of shipping real ML systems, not just research
- Clear contact/connection information
- Thought leadership that demonstrates big-picture thinking

## Secondary Persona: The Industry Peer

**Name:** Jordan (Fellow Engineer/Researcher)

**Demographics:**
- Age range: 25-45
- Professional background: Software engineer, ML engineer, researcher, or technical founder
- Technical expertise level: High - deeply technical, can appreciate implementation details
- Industry: Tech, research, or adjacent fields

**Goals:**
- Learn from someone with 15+ years of ML experience
- Discover interesting projects and approaches
- Connect with potential collaborators or mentors
- Stay current on AI/ML trends from a practitioner's perspective

**Pain Points:**
- Too much AI/ML content is either pure research or pure hype
- Hard to find authentic voices with long-term ML experience
- Want to learn from real implementation details, not just high-level concepts
- Seeking community and connection in the ML engineering space

**User Journey:**
1. **Discovery** - Finds site through technical content, GitHub, social media, or peer recommendation
2. **Deep Dive** - Reads project showcases, especially technical implementation details
3. **Learning** - Returns for thought leadership content and new projects
4. **Engagement** - May reach out for collaboration, questions, or just to connect
5. **Advocacy** - Shares interesting content with their network

**What they need from the site:**
- Technical depth and implementation details
- Honest discussion of trade-offs and challenges
- Links to GitHub and other technical work
- Perspective on ML engineering practice, not just theory

## Tertiary Persona: The Curious Visitor

**Name:** Sam (General Tech Enthusiast)

**Demographics:**
- Age range: 22-60
- Professional background: Varied - may be in tech, adjacent fields, or completely different industries
- Technical expertise level: Low to medium - interested in technology but not deeply technical
- Industry: Varied

**Goals:**
- Learn about AI/ML in an accessible way
- Understand what working in AI/ML is actually like
- Discover interesting projects and perspectives
- Be inspired by someone's work and interests

**Pain Points:**
- Most technical content is either too simple or completely inaccessible
- Hard to find authentic perspectives that aren't trying to sell something
- Want to understand the big picture without getting lost in jargon
- Interested in the person behind the work, not just the resume

**User Journey:**
1. **Discovery** - Finds site through social media, personal connection, or casual browsing
2. **Exploration** - Drawn in by whimsical design and accessible writing
3. **Understanding** - Learns about AI/ML projects through big-picture explanations
4. **Connection** - Appreciates personal interests and hobbies alongside technical work
5. **Return visits** - Comes back when new content appears in their feed

**What they need from the site:**
- Accessible explanations that start with the big picture
- Visual interest and engaging design
- Personal elements that humanize the technical work
- Clear navigation and mobile-friendly experience

## Design Implications

**For All Personas:**
- Mobile-first design (everyone browses on phones)
- Clear, immediate understanding of who this person is and what they do
- Easy access to portfolio, GitHub, and contact information
- Balance of technical depth and accessibility

**For Hiring Managers:**
- Clear evidence of 15+ years shipping production ML systems
- Breadth showcase: generative AI, personalization, architecture, signal processing
- Verifiable project details that support resume claims
- Professional presentation with authentic personality
- Easy verification via LinkedIn/GitHub links

**For Technical Decision-Makers (Clients):**
- Prominent showcase of significant projects with measurable impact
- Thought leadership that demonstrates strategic thinking
- Evidence of bridging technical depth with business outcomes
- Professional but not corporate feel

**For Industry Peers:**
- Deep technical content available without overwhelming the main experience
- Links to GitHub, open-source work, and technical details
- Honest discussion of challenges and trade-offs
- Community/connection opportunities

**For Curious Visitors:**
- Whimsical, inviting design that makes technology feel approachable
- Big-picture explanations before diving into details
- Personal interests and hobbies visible alongside technical work
- Engaging visual design with pops of color and creative layout
